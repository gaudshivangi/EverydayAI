Understanding AI, Responsible Use, and Digital Literacy

Do's and Don'ts of Using AI

DO's — Responsible AI Use

Understand AI Limitations

AI tools assist decision-making but do not replace human judgment. Always verify critical information.

Use AI Ethically

Use AI tools for learning, productivity, and creativity without harming others or spreading misinformation.

Protect Personal Data

Avoid sharing sensitive personal information with AI applications or online platforms.

Cross-Check AI Outputs

Verify AI-generated content using trusted sources, especially for education and health topics.

DON'Ts — Avoid These

Don't Trust AI Blindly

AI can generate incorrect or biased information. Always apply critical thinking before acting on it.

Don't Share Confidential Data

Never upload passwords, personal documents, or private details into AI tools.

Don't Use AI for Plagiarism

Avoid copying AI-generated content directly for assignments or work without understanding it.

Don't Spread Misinformation

Always recheck facts before sharing AI-generated text, images, or videos online.

Safety Practices for Users

Strong Passwords

Use unique, complex passwords for AI accounts to prevent unauthorized access to your data.

Data Privacy

Review privacy settings on AI platforms. Understand what data is being collected and stored.

Avoid Scams

Be cautious of AI-generated phishing emails or deepfake videos asking for money or personal info.

Screen Time

Maintain a healthy balance. Excessive reliance on AI tools can hinder critical thinking skills.

The Impact of AI on Job Interviews

In recent years, companies have started using AI to screen job candidates. This case study explores how AI algorithms analyze facial expressions, tone of voice, and word choice during video interviews. While this increases efficiency for companies, it raises questions about fairness and bias.
