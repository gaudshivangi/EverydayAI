Frequently Asked Questions

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is technology that enables machines to simulate human intelligence — learning from data, recognizing patterns, making decisions, and understanding language. It powers tools like Google Maps, voice assistants, and recommendation systems.

AI is present in your daily routine — YouTube and Netflix recommendations, Google Maps traffic prediction, face unlock on smartphones, email spam filters, autocorrect, and online shopping suggestions all use AI behind the scenes.

No technical knowledge is required. Most AI tools today are designed for everyday users with simple interfaces. Tools like ChatGPT, Grammarly, and Google Gemini can be used by anyone with basic computer skills.

AI tools are generally safe when used responsibly. Avoid sharing sensitive personal information, use trusted platforms, and always verify important AI-generated content with reliable sources before acting on it.

Ethical AI means using AI fairly, transparently, and responsibly. This includes being aware of algorithm bias, protecting user privacy, avoiding misuse of AI-generated content, and thinking critically before trusting AI outputs.

Never share passwords, ID numbers, or sensitive documents with AI tools. Read the privacy policy of any app you use, stick to well-known platforms, and be cautious about what photos or personal information you upload.

AI can generate incorrect information — a phenomenon called "hallucination." This happens because AI predicts responses based on patterns in training data rather than truly understanding facts. Always verify AI answers with trusted sources.

AI can help students by explaining difficult concepts simply, proofreading writing, translating languages, solving math step-by-step, and organizing study schedules. However, it should be used as a learning aid — not a replacement for your own thinking.

A deepfake is AI-generated fake video or image that makes a real person appear to say or do something they never did. To spot one — look for unnatural blinking, blurry edges around the face, or inconsistent lighting. Always verify shocking content before sharing.

AI is changing jobs rather than simply replacing them. While some repetitive tasks are being automated, new AI-related roles are being created. Skills like creativity, critical thinking, and empathy remain uniquely human — learning to work with AI is the best approach.
